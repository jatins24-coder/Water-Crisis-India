India's Water Crisis: A Growing Catastrophe

India is facing one of the worst water crises in its history. Millions of people are suffering due to water scarcity, and many regions are running dry. From agriculture to daily consumption, the demand for water is ever-increasing while our sources are rapidly depleting.

According to NITI Aayog, nearly 600 million Indians face high to extreme water stress, and about 200,000 people die every year due to inadequate access to safe water. By 2030, the country's water demand is projected to be twice the available supply, implying severe water scarcity for hundreds of millions.

Call to Action

India must adopt aggressive water conservation practices, implement rainwater harvesting, treat and reuse wastewater, and reform irrigation techniques in agriculture. Citizens must also be made aware and involved in the effort.
